# 820 Payment Order / Remittance Advice

**30-Second Explanation**: The file an employer sends to the insurance company saying, "Here is the monthly premium payment for all my employees."

**2-Minute Explanation**: While the 834 tells the Payer *who* is enrolled, the 820 tells the Payer *that they have been paid* for those enrollments. An employer with 10,000 employees sends a single massive wire transfer to Humana for $5 Million. The 820 file is sent alongside it, breaking down exactly how that $5 Million is divided among the 10,000 employees' monthly premiums. 

**Purpose**: To reconcile premium payments between sponsors (employers) and payers.

**Who sends it**: Employers, Government Exchanges.

**Who receives it**: Payers.

**Input**: Premium amounts, employee IDs, coverage periods.

**Output**: Updates the Payer's billing system to show premiums are paid up to date.

**Common Errors**: 
- The 820 payment file arrives, but the corresponding 834 enrollment file failed. The Payer now has money for members that don't exist in their system.

**Real Project Example**: Integration engineers often have to build cross-transaction validations to ensure that 820 processing logic checks Transaction Manager to verify the 834 was successfully processed first.

**Interview Questions**:
- *What is the difference between an 835 and an 820?* (Answer: Both deal with money. 835 is the Payer paying the Provider for *claims*. 820 is the Employer paying the Payer for *premiums*).
