# 834 Benefit Enrollment

**30-Second Explanation**: The file that tells the insurance company who is actually insured. 

**2-Minute Explanation**: When you get a job, you fill out health insurance forms. Your employer's HR system batches up all employee choices and sends a massive 834 file to the Payer (e.g., Aetna). This file tells Aetna: "Add John to the Gold Plan, add his wife as a dependent, and cancel Sarah's plan because she quit." Without the 834, the Payer's system has no idea who their members are.

**Purpose**: To synchronize member rosters between employers/exchanges and the insurance company.

**Who sends it**: Employers, HR Platforms (Workday), Government Exchanges (Healthcare.gov).

**Who receives it**: Payers.

**Input**: Employee demographics, plan selections, dependents.

**Output**: Creation or termination of members in the Payer's core system.

**Common Errors**: 
- Sending an 834 to terminate an employee, but the Payer's system fails to process it, resulting in the Payer continuing to pay claims for an ex-employee.
- Bad demographic mapping causing duplicate member IDs.

**Real Project Example**: At Edifecs, 834 mapping is notoriously complex because it dictates the entire downstream lifecycle. If the 834 map corrupts a Date of Birth, every subsequent 270 (Eligibility) and 837 (Claim) for that member will fail with a DOB-mismatch.

**Interview Questions**:
- *Why is the 834 considered the "source of truth" transaction?* (Answer: Because all other transactions (270, 837, 276) rely on the Payer's database having accurate member data, which is populated exclusively by the 834).
