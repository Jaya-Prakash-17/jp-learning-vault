# 276 Claim Status Inquiry

**30-Second Explanation**: A digital ping sent by a provider to a payer asking, "What is the status of the claim I submitted last week?"

**2-Minute Explanation**: Instead of having a hospital billing clerk call a payer's call center and wait on hold to ask about a claim, the hospital's software automatically generates an X12 276 file. This file contains the provider's NPI, the patient's Member ID, and the specific claim details. The payer's system reads this file, looks up the claim in their adjudication engine, and instantly replies with a 277 response.

**Purpose**: To programmatically track the processing or adjudication status of a previously submitted claim (837).

**Who sends it**: Providers (Hospitals, Clinics).

**Who receives it**: Payers (Insurance Companies, Medicare).

**Input**: Patient demographics, Provider NPI, Date of Service, Claim Charge Amount.

**Output**: Triggers a corresponding 277 Claim Status Response.

**Common Errors**: 
- Missing or invalid Member ID.
- Missing Provider NPI.
- Requesting status for a claim that hasn't even entered the payer's system yet.

**Real Project Example**: At Humana, I modernized the 276 ingestion pipeline. We received 276 inquiries in real-time, parsed the X12 payload via Edifecs, queried the backend CORE APIs for the status, and mapped the response into a valid 277 within 2.5 seconds.

**Interview Questions**:
- *Why are 276 transactions often processed synchronously (real-time) while 837s are batch?* (Answer: 276 is an inquiry requiring an immediate answer, often while a patient is waiting or an accountant is working a ledger. 837s are massive dumps of data that take days to adjudicate.)
