# 835 Electronic Remittance Advice (ERA)

**30-Second Explanation**: The receipt and explanation of payment. It tells the provider exactly which claims were paid, denied, or adjusted, and why.

**2-Minute Explanation**: After a payer processes an 837 claim, they send money to the hospital's bank via EFT. But the bank deposit just says "$50,000". The hospital needs to know *which* patients that $50,000 covers. The payer sends an 835 ERA file simultaneously. The hospital's software automatically reads the 835 and posts the payments to individual patient accounts. It uses CARC and RARC codes to explain deductions (e.g., "Patient owes $50 copay").

**Purpose**: Automated payment posting and reconciliation for providers.

**Who sends it**: Payers.

**Who receives it**: Providers.

**Input**: Final adjudication results from the payer's core system.

**Output**: Feeds directly into the provider's accounting/EHR system.

**Common Errors**: 
- The 835 doesn't balance (Total Paid + Adjustments != Total Billed).
- The Trace Number (TRN) on the 835 doesn't match the Trace Number on the bank's EFT, preventing reconciliation.

**Real Project Example**: When mapping data for Payers, ensuring that the Claim ID submitted on the 837 is perfectly echoed back on the 835 is critical; otherwise, the hospital's system cannot auto-post the payment and humans have to manually review it.

**Interview Questions**:
- *How does a provider link the 835 file to the actual money in their bank account?* (Answer: Using the TRN - Trace Number segment, which is present in both the 835 payload and the bank's EFT wire transfer).
- *What is a CARC code?* (Answer: Claim Adjustment Reason Code. It explains why a claim wasn't paid in full).
