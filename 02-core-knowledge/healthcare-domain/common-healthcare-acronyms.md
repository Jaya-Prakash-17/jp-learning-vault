# Common Healthcare Acronyms

**Business Purpose**: Fluency in these acronyms is mandatory to pass technical interviews at companies like Cotiviti, Optum, and Gainwell. You must speak the language of the domain.

- **NPI**: National Provider Identifier (10-digit ID for doctors/hospitals).
- **PHI**: Protected Health Information (Sensitive data regulated by HIPAA).
- **HIPAA**: Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (Federal law mandating security and EDI standards).
- **EHR/EMR**: Electronic Health Record (Epic, Cerner - where doctors type clinical notes).
- **CMS**: Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (The federal agency that dictates most healthcare rules).
- **EDI**: Electronic Data Interchange (The format, like X12, used to exchange data).
- **SNIP**: Strategic National Implementation Process (The 7 levels of validation for X12 files. E.g., SNIP 1 is syntax, SNIP 2 is structural).
- **COB**: Coordination of Benefits (Figuring out who pays first when a patient has two insurance plans).
- **CARC/RARC**: Claim Adjustment Reason Codes (Used in 835s to explain exactly why money was deducted).
- **EFT**: Electronic Funds Transfer (The actual bank payment).
