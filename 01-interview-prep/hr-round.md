# HR / Recruiter Round

## 1. The Need (Business Purpose)
- **The Need**: Technical skills get you the job, but cultural fit and salary alignment get you the offer. Companies use HR rounds to screen out candidates who are flight risks, toxic to team culture, or outside the budget.

## 2. Core Concepts
- **STAR Method**: Situation, Task, Action, Result. The gold standard for answering behavioral questions.
- **Salary Negotiation**: Anchoring, knowing the market rate, and avoiding giving the first number if possible.
- **Cultural Alignment**: Demonstrating empathy, ego-less engineering, and an eagerness to learn.

## 3. Basic Daily Workflows
- **The Elevator Pitch**: Delivering a tight, 60-second summary of who you are, what you've done, and what you are looking for (Tell me about yourself).
- **Connecting the Dots**: Explicitly tying your past projects (Humana/Colorado) to the bullet points in their job description.

## 4. Advanced Scenarios
- **Navigating the "Why are you leaving?" Trap**: Framing the departure around *seeking growth* rather than escaping a bad manager or complaining about your current company.
- **Handling Salary Pressure**: Navigating aggressive recruiters pushing for an exact number by focusing on Total Target Compensation (Base + Bonus + Equity) and iterating that you are open to competitive market rates.

## Interview Question Deep Dive

### Q: "Why are you looking to leave your current role at Edifecs/Cotiviti?"

**30-Second Answer**: I’ve gained incredible experience modernizing massive healthcare EDI pipelines, like reducing Humana's transaction turnaround by 80%. However, I am now looking for a role that allows me to push further into cloud-native architectures (AWS/Docker) and agentic AI systems, moving beyond traditional on-prem middleware.

**2-Minute Answer**: At Edifecs, I successfully transitioned from my academic AI background into enterprise healthcare integration. I own end-to-end workflows processing 300K+ daily transactions. I've mastered the domain—understanding the nuances of X12, HIPAA, and complex mapping. But my passion lies at the intersection of healthcare data and modern cloud/AI engineering. My current role caps my exposure to those modern infrastructure challenges. I want to bring my deep domain expertise to a team that is building next-generation platforms.

**Deep Dive Answer**: The future of healthcare isn't just moving X12 files faster; it's using AI and modern API orchestration to interpret that data in real-time. I want to build systems where large language models or agentic frameworks can automatically resolve "Member Not Found" EDI errors by querying external demographic APIs before the claim is ever rejected. I am looking for a company whose roadmap aligns with that level of innovation.

**Real Example**: During the Humana 276/277 modernization, I pushed our legacy components to the absolute limit, tuning the Native Translator to handle 65K records/hour. It showed me that to scale further, we need distributed, containerized microservices.

**Follow-up Questions**:
- *Interviewer*: "What are your salary expectations?"
- *Interviewer*: "Are you comfortable moving away from Edifecs-specific tools into raw coding?"
