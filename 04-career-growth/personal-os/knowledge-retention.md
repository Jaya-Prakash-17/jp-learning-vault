# Knowledge Retention Strategy

Your memory is volatile. The `jp-learning-vault` acts as your hard drive. To ensure you stay "interview-ready" year-round without cramming, use this passive retention system.

## The "Interview or Forget" Rule
If a piece of knowledge cannot be formatted into a Cheat Sheet command or a 2-Minute Interview Question, it is probably fluff. Force all your notes into actionable formats.

## The 10-Minute Monday Routine
Spaced repetition is the key to retaining complex architectural concepts. 

Every Monday morning, while drinking your coffee, pick exactly **one** file from the `01-interview-prep/` folder at random.
- Read the question.
- Cover the screen.
- Try to deliver the "2-Minute Answer" out loud.
- If you stumble, read the answer, and you're done.

This takes 10 minutes a week, but guarantees that by the end of the year, you have practiced your most critical interview answers over 50 times.

## Pre-Interview Cramming
When a recruiter calls, you don't need to panic-search Google.
1. Read the `99-cheatsheets/` on your phone on the train or in the lobby.
2. Review the `03-project-evidence/` files to ensure your STAR stories (Humana/Colorado) are loaded in your short-term memory.
